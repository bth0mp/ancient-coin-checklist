## Coverage

The images span the Byzantine Empire from its early period (Anastasius I, 491-518) through to the middle Byzantine period (Constantine VII, 913-959), covering approximately 500 years of numismatic history. Featured denominations include:

- **Solidus** - The standard gold coin of the Byzantine Empire
- **Follis** - Large bronze denomination

Featured emperors:
- Anastasius I (491-518) - Currency reformer
- Justinian I (527-565) - Greatest Byzantine emperor
- Heraclius (610-641) - Defender against Persia and early Islam
- Justinian II (685-695, 705-711) - First to depict Christ on coinage
- Theophilus (829-842) - Last Iconoclast emperor
- Constantine VII (913-959) - Scholar emperor
