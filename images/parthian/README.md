## About the Parthian Empire

The Parthian Empire (247 BC – 224 AD), also known as the Arsacid Empire, was a major Iranian political and cultural power in ancient Persia. Parthian coinage was primarily silver, with the main denominations being:

- **Tetradrachm** (~16g) - Minted only at Seleucia
- **Drachm** (~3-4g) - The standard silver coin
- **Bronze denominations** - For local trade

Key characteristics of Parthian coins:
- Obverse: King's portrait, often wearing diadem or tiara
- Reverse: Typically shows the "archer king" seated on an omphalos (sacred stone), holding a bow
- Greek inscriptions (showing Hellenistic influence)
- Later coins show more Iranian cultural elements

Notable rulers represented:
- **Mithridates II "the Great"** (124-91 BC) - The greatest Parthian emperor
- **Sinatruces** (77-70 BC) - Known for gold coinage
- **Osroes I** (109-129 AD) - Late Parthian ruler
