## About the Greco-Bactrian Kingdom

The Greco-Bactrian Kingdom (c. 256-125 BC) was a Hellenistic kingdom in Central Asia covering parts of modern Afghanistan, Pakistan, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. It was founded when Diodotus I declared independence from the Seleucid Empire.

The kingdom is famous for producing some of the finest Hellenistic coins, with remarkably realistic portraiture. Notable rulers include:
- **Euthydemos I** - Founded the Euthydemid dynasty
- **Demetrios I** - Expanded into India, depicted with elephant headdress
- **Eucratides I** - Known for the largest gold coin from antiquity (20-stater)
- **Menander I** - The most famous Indo-Greek king
